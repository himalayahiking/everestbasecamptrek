Frequently Asked Questions

Yes, trekking in Nepal is generally safe, especially on popular routes. Hiring a guide and staying informed about weather and conditions enhances safety.

Difficulty varies by route. Some treks like Everest Base Camp or Annapurna Circuit require good fitness, while others are moderate and beginner-friendly.

Independent trekking is allowed in many regions. However, in restricted areas like Upper Mustang or Manaslu, a guide and group are mandatory.

Nepal offers breathtaking landscapes, rich cultures, and unforgettable mountain experiences, making it a top destination for trekkers worldwide.

Trekking costs vary. Budget treks are affordable, while remote regions or luxury lodges cost more. Permit fees and guide services add to the total.

As of 2023, solo trekking is restricted in most regions. You must register with a licensed trekking agency and hire a guide for your safety.

A moderate level of fitness is usually enough. Training with hikes, cardio, and strength building beforehand is highly recommended.

Most treks offer teahouse lodging—basic but cozy. In remote or less-traveled areas, tented camping may be necessary.

Build your fitness, research your route, pack wisely, and consult with a reputable trekking agency for permits and logistics.

The Everest Base Camp trail is Nepal’s most iconic trek, followed closely by the Annapurna Circuit and Langtang Valley routes.
